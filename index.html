Technical Specifications

Engineered for Precision

The polar planimeter measures irregular planar areas by tracing their perimeter — a purely mechanical solution to a calculus problem.

2
Articulated Arms

A fixed pole arm anchors the instrument while the tracer arm follows the boundary of any shape.

360°
Rotation Range

The pivot joint allows full rotation, enabling measurement of any closed curve regardless of shape.

±1%
Accuracy

A calibrated measuring wheel rolling perpendicular to the tracer arm accumulates displacement with high precision.

∮
Green's Theorem

Area equals the line integral of the boundary — the planimeter computes this mechanically as you trace.

0 W
Power Required

Entirely mechanical. No electronics, no batteries — pure geometry and precision machined tolerances.

cm²
Output Unit

Reads directly in area. Multiply by the squared map scale factor to obtain real-world area.

Mechanism

How It Works

01

Anchor the Pole

The pole arm is pinned at a fixed point outside the figure. It stays stationary throughout the measurement.

02

Trace the Boundary

The tracer point is guided carefully along the full perimeter of the shape, returning to the start point.

03

The Wheel Integrates

A measuring wheel mounted perpendicular to the tracer arm rolls and slips, mechanically computing the line integral of perpendicular displacement.

04

Read the Area

The vernier drum displays total area directly. No computation needed — the instrument has already integrated for you.
